Marinated Grilled Shrimp

Description

Marinated Grilled Shrimp is a dish that brings the vibrant flavors of the coast to your plate. The shrimp are first marinated in a mixture of fresh lemon juice, garlic, and aromatic herbs like parsley or cilantro. This marinade infuses the shrimp with a bright, tangy flavor, while a touch of olive oil adds richness and helps the seasonings cling to every bite. Once marinated, the shrimp are skewered and grilled over an open flame, where they develop a slightly charred, smoky finish. The heat caramelizes the shrimp's natural sweetness, leaving the outside crisp and the inside tender and juicy. Each bite delivers a burst of citrusy, garlicky goodness, perfectly balanced by the subtle smokiness from the grill. It's a light yet flavorful dish, perfect for pairing with fresh salads or grilled vegetables, making it an ideal choice for a summer meal.

Ingredients

  • ⅓ cup olive oil
  • ¼ cup tomato sauce
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 pounds fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 6 skewers

Steps

  1. Stir together olive oil, tomato sauce, garlic, and red wine vinegar in a large bowl. Season with basil, salt, and cayenne pepper.
  2. Add shrimp to the bowl; stir until evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes to 1 hour, stirring once or twice.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium heat. Lightly oil the grate.
  4. Thread shrimp onto skewers, piercing once near the tail and once near the head. Discard marinade.
  5. Cook shrimp on the preheated grill until opaque, 2 to 3 minutes per side.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!
